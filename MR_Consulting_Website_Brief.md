# MR Consulting LLC — Website Brief for Claude Code

## Project Overview

Build a professional consulting website for **MR Consulting LLC**, a solo electrical engineering consultancy based in the Boston/Watertown, MA area serving clients throughout the United States. The primary audience is VPs of Engineering, CTOs, and founders at early-stage and mid-size medical device companies in the Boston/Cambridge corridor and beyond.

---

## Business Identity

- **Legal Name:** MR Consulting LLC
- **Location:** Watertown, MA (Boston area)
- **Service Area:** United States
- **Market Focus:** Medical device companies — early-stage startups through mid-size
- **Geographic Sweet Spot:** Boston/Cambridge corridor (Kendall Square, Route 128 belt)

---

## Positioning Statement

An experienced electrical engineer and medical device specialist offering hands-on technical consulting — from circuit design and firmware to manufacturing readiness and standards compliance. With nearly two decades of product development experience and a background in both engineering leadership and operations, I help medical device companies build products that work, that can be made, and that will hold up under regulatory scrutiny.

**Tagline concept:** *"Engineering expertise with leadership perspective — from first schematic to manufacturing floor."*

**Core positioning distinction:** This is not a generalist consultant who also knows medical devices. This is a medical device engineer who can handle the full electrical stack — and who has operated at both the individual contributor and director level.

---

## About / Bio Content

**Education:** B.S. Electrical and Computer Engineering, Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI), 2006

**Experience:** Nearly two decades of engineering experience. Most recently, approximately a decade specializing in infrared-based temperature measurement at a medical device company in the Boston area.

**Background breadth:** Prior experience in defense and aviation — deliberately kept in the background rather than featured, but available as a credibility note if relevant.

**Leadership background:** Has operated as both an individual contributor and in management roles:
- Director of Engineering (engineering teams up to ~10 people)
- Operations Manager (teams up to ~25 people)
- Hiring, performance management, cross-functional project leadership
- Understands both the engineer's perspective and leadership's priorities
- Has sat at the table with leadership teams; can engage client executives as a peer, not just as a contractor

**Key differentiator:** Bridges the gap between design and manufacturing. Understands how upstream engineering decisions affect what happens on the manufacturing floor — a rare combination.

---

## Website Goals

1. Establish professional credibility with medical device VPs and CTOs
2. Clearly communicate the services/expertise mix without over-complicating it
3. Make it easy for a prospective client to quickly understand fit and take the next step (contact)
4. Reflect the positioning: expert-level, practical, experienced — not a large firm, not a freelancer marketplace profile

---

## Tone & Style Direction

- Professional but not corporate — this is a solo practitioner with deep expertise, not a 200-person firm
- Direct and confident — no hedging, no over-explaining
- Technically credible — the copy should feel like it was written by someone who has actually done this work
- Clean and readable — the audience is busy engineering and business leaders; respect their time
- Warm enough to invite contact, but not overly casual

---

## Pages / Structure (Suggested)

1. **Home** — Hero with positioning statement/tagline, brief "who this is for," CTA to contact
2. **About** — Bio, background, education, leadership story, the differentiator (design-to-manufacturing bridge)
3. **Services** — Clean breakdown of the three service areas with enough detail to establish credibility without being a spec sheet
4. **Contact** — Simple contact form or direct email/LinkedIn link; location (Boston/Watertown, MA); note that work is done nationally

---

## What to Avoid

- Don't overemphasize defense/aviation background — it's real but not the lead
- Don't position as a "quality consultant" — the regulatory work is engineering documentation support
- Don't claim design for fully autonomous manufacturing lines — project management of those engagements is fine, execution is not
- Don't make it look like a large firm — authenticity as a solo expert is a strength, not a weakness
- Don't over-clutter with every technology ever touched — keep the focus on the medical device electrical engineering story
