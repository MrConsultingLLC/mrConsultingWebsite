Services

The full electrical stack — plus the judgment to use it.

Three core service areas, focused on the work medical device teams actually need: building it, making it compliant, and getting it to manufacturing.

01 — Core

Electrical Engineering

End-to-end electrical development, from the first schematic to working, tested hardware and the firmware that drives it.

Circuit design for medical devices

  • Analog and mixed-signal — precision signal chains, sensor front-ends, and A-to-D conversion
  • Patient isolation — isolation barriers, isolated signal paths, and leakage-current management per IEC 60601-1
  • Low-power and battery-operated design
  • PCB layout, component selection, and qualification
  • Charging circuits and wireless power — Qi / inductive charging
  • Sensor integration — IR temperature, thermistors, thermocouples, accelerometers
  • Wireless connectivity — Bluetooth (module-level)

Firmware & software

  • Embedded firmware in C
  • Microchip PIC and AVR microcontrollers
  • Supporting software in Python, LabVIEW, and C#
  • Electrical automation design and implementation
02 — Specialization

Medical Device Specialization

Designing and documenting to the standards medical devices live by — informed by years of doing it inside a regulated environment.

Standards-driven design

  • IEC 60601-1 — general safety and essential performance
  • IEC 60601-1-2, 4th Edition + Amendment 2 — electromagnetic compatibility (EMC)
  • Risk management support per ISO 14971:2019
  • Manufacturing process development within ISO 13485 quality systems
  • Design for Manufacturability (DFM)

Documentation support

  • Audit readiness — DHF review and technical documentation preparation
  • Technical file authorship
  • Design History File (DHF) authorship and review
  • Risk Management File authorship
  • Manufacturing process development documentation

Scope note: This is engineering documentation preparation support — not regulatory strategy or quality-system consulting.

03 — Delivery

Project & Program Support

Keeping development moving — across the lifecycle, and especially across the hand-off from design to production.

Program leadership

  • Project management across the full product development lifecycle
  • Design-to-manufacturing transition
  • Cross-functional coordination between engineering, quality, and operations

Automation coordination

  • Automation project management — scoping and vendor coordination

Scope note: Designing fully autonomous manufacturing lines is specialist territory. Project-managing those engagements — scoping, vendor selection, and coordination — is squarely in scope.
